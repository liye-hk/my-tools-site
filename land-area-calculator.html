Land Measurement Units Explained

Land measurement units vary depending on the country and region. Below are some commonly used units in land calculations.

Square Feet (Sq Ft)

Square feet is one of the most commonly used land measurement units, especially in urban areas and real estate markets. It represents the total area of land in square feet.

Square Meter (Sq M)

Square meters are widely used in international land measurement systems. Many countries use square meters for property documentation and land registration.

Katha

Katha is a traditional land measurement unit used in India, Nepal, and Bangladesh. However, the exact value of a katha can vary depending on the region.

Bigha

Bigha is another traditional land unit commonly used in rural areas. Farmers and landowners often use bigha when buying or selling agricultural land.

Acre

An acre is a widely recognized international land measurement unit, often used for measuring large plots, farms, and agricultural fields.

Hectare

A hectare is part of the metric system and is commonly used in government land records and international land measurement.
