What is Bigha?

Bigha is a traditional unit of land measurement widely used in South Asia. The exact value of one bigha can vary depending on the state or region. For example, the size of a bigha in Uttar Pradesh may be slightly different from the size used in Bihar, Rajasthan, or West Bengal.

Despite these variations, bigha remains one of the most commonly used land measurement units in rural areas. Farmers and landowners frequently use bigha when buying, selling, or calculating farmland.

What is an Acre?

An acre is an internationally recognized unit used for measuring land area. It is widely used in real estate, agriculture, and property documentation around the world. One acre is equal to 43,560 square feet or approximately 4046.86 square meters.

Because acre is part of the international measurement system, it is commonly used in government land records and official property documents.
