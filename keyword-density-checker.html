What Is Keyword Density?

Keyword density refers to the percentage of times a specific keyword appears in a piece of content compared to the total number of words. Search engines use this information to determine the relevance of a page for a particular search query. For example, if your article has 1000 words and your main keyword appears 20 times, the keyword density would be 2%.

Most SEO experts recommend maintaining a keyword density between 1% and 3%. This ensures that the keyword appears naturally in the content without being overused.

Why Keyword Density Is Important for SEO

Search engines like Google analyze keywords to understand what a webpage is about. If the right keywords appear naturally in the content, the page has a better chance of ranking for relevant searches. However, keyword stuffing can negatively impact rankings. That is why it is important to monitor keyword density while writing SEO content.
